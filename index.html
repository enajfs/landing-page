The problem

Your inbox is costing you
more than just time.

Every workday begins with orientation. Every transition back into the inbox repeats it. And somewhere in the middle, something important slips.

01

The stolen hour

Every workday begins with 30–60 minutes of inbox catch-up before any real work begins. That's roughly 12.5% of payroll spent on orientation, not production. And it repeats — after every meeting, post-lunch, end of day.

02

The forgotten reply

You started a reply, got distracted, and the draft sat unsent. Or you read an email meaning to come back to it — and didn't. By Thursday you've dropped three things you meant to follow up on. This isn't a time cost. It's a trust cost.

What HeadStart does

Five things.
Done consistently.

HeadStart doesn't draft replies or act on your behalf. It shows you what matters, tracks what you've dropped, and shows up again tomorrow if you were too busy today.

01

Surfaces only what matters

Reads your mailbox, filters out commercial noise and no-reply senders, then classifies the rest — action-required, meeting, status update, or FYI. You see only what's worth your attention.

02

Tracks follow-through deterministically

For each action-required item, HeadStart checks your Sent and Drafts folders via your mail provider's API. It reports the facts: pending, draft started, or responded. No AI guessing — just mailbox truth.

03

Delivers at 06:55, every morning

A push notification arrives on your phone at 06:55. One tap opens the briefing. The push also functions as a daily reminder the product exists — preventing the silent churn that kills useful tools.

04

Available on-demand throughout the day

The briefing URL — or PWA on your home screen — generates a fresh delta-since-last-view briefing every time you open it. No scheduled interrupts during the day. Your workday is unpredictable; HeadStart respects that.

05

Self-heals across days

If you're too busy to engage today, yesterday's unhandled items appear in tomorrow's morning briefing automatically. There's no scenario where things slip because you were too busy.

HeadStart works even on the days you don't.

FAQ

Honest answers.

Will HeadStart read all my email?

HeadStart reads your inbox to classify and surface what needs your attention. Commercial senders and no-reply addresses are filtered out before classification. It does not store email content beyond what's needed to generate your briefing.

Is my data safe?

HeadStart is built so it can run on your infrastructure when you're ready. We're not making broad claims about data sovereignty at this stage — we'll walk you through the specifics when we connect.

What if I miss the morning notification?

No problem. Yesterday's unhandled items show up in tomorrow's morning briefing automatically. You can also open the briefing on-demand any time during the day and get a fresh view. The product is forgiving of real workdays.

Does HeadStart work with Outlook and Gmail?

Yes — HeadStart is being built for both Microsoft 365 / Outlook and Google Workspace / Gmail. The pain it solves isn't platform-specific, and neither is the solution.

Will it sort or reorganize my inbox?

No. HeadStart reads and presents — it does not archive, label, sort, or reorganize anything. Your inbox stays exactly as you left it. HeadStart only changes what you see, not what's there.
